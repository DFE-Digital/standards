Meeting this standard

You must report compliance with this standard in the Service health check hub.

To meet this standard, you must:

Publish a statement

You must publish an accessibility statement in your product before it is accessed by any users.

The statement must be accessed from the footer of all pages from a link with the text "Accessibility" in accordance with published guidance.

You can use the templates in the accessibility manual to make this easier.

Detail WCAG criterion failures

The statement must include details of all known WCAG (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines) criterion failures.

This transparency is crucial for users with disabilities to be aware of any potential accessibility barriers they may encounter. The process for reporting and managing these accessibility issues is covered by a separate standard, ensuring a systematic approach to addressing and resolving accessibility challenges.

Mitigation and resolution actions

You must state clearly what actions you are taking to mitigate or resolve these accessibility issues, including any expected timelines for resolution. This provides users with assurance that their concerns are being actively addressed and that improvements are being made to enhance their user experience.

Update regularly

The statement must be updated within 2 months following any audits or additional accessibility testing. This timely update ensures that the information remains current and reflects the most recent findings and actions taken to improve accessibility.

Complete annual review

Even if no interim testing or audits are conducted, the statement should be reviewed and updated at least every 12 months. This regular review process ensures ongoing compliance with accessibility standards and demonstrates a commitment to maintaining an accessible digital product for all users.
